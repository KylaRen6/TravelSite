Explore Germany!

Welcome To Your New Favorite Travel Destination

Germany is a beautiful country in central Europe with thousands of years of rich history. It also has stunning nature everywhere with mountains, rivers, lakes, and huge forests to explore. In Germany you can take a boat down the Danube and photograph historical castles along the way, then stop for a delicious dinner of bratwurst, potato pancakes, and spaetzle. Germany is also full of modern cities like Munich and Berlin where you can visit museums and historical sites. If you want to visit somewhere else, simply hop on the train system that traverses the entire country and will even take you into nearby countries like Austria and France.

Must See Locations:

  1. The Brandenburg Gate



    2. The Brandenburg Gate was built between 1788 and 1791 by King Frederick William II. On top of the Gate sits the Quadriga-a chariot pulled by three horses. The Quadriga is a copy however, as the original was damaged during bombings in World War II. When Germany was split between the East and the West in the years following the war, no one from either side was permitted to visit the Gate, and when the Berlin Wall fell in 1989 it became a symbol of reuinification and peace.

  3. Cologne Cathedral



    4. Construction of the Colonge Cathedral began in 1248 and was not completed until 1880, over six hundred years later. The work took place over several stages and it is now considered to be a masterpiece of gothic architecture. It is a High Gothic five-aisled basilica and the entire structure is over five hundred feet tall and covered in intricate, hand carved designs.

  4. The Danube River



    5. The Danube river begins in Germany and flows southeast for nearly two thousand miles where it empties into the Black Sea. Along the way, it passes through Germany, Austria, Slovakia, Hungary, Croatia, Serbia, Bulgaria, Romania, Moldova and finally Ukrain. Many river cruises are offered along the Danube, during which many castles can be seen along its banks. It has historically formed boundaries between kingdoms as well as served to move commercial goods throughout Europe. In modern times it continues play an important role in trade as well as in hydroelectric power.
